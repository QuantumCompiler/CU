The Rubik's Cube

About

The Rubik's Cube, invented by Ernő Rubik in 1974, is a 3D puzzle with six colored faces. Solving it involves aligning all colors on each face. Despite its 43 quintillion configurations, it can be solved in 20 moves or fewer. It symbolizes intellectual challenge and is popular in education and competitions.

World Records

The world records for the Rubik's cube can be seen below.

  • Single Solve: The fastest single solve record is held by Yusheng Du of China, with a time of 3.47 seconds, set on November 24, 2018.
  • Average of Five Solves: The fastest average of five solves record is held by Tymon Kolasiński of Poland, with an average time of 4.33 seconds, set on June 11, 2023.
  • One-Handed Solve: The fastest one-handed solve record is held by Max Park of the United States, with a time of 6.20 seconds, set on August 11, 2023.
  • Blindfolded Solve: The fastest blindfolded solve record is held by Max Hilliard of the United States, with a time of 12.10 seconds, set on January 25, 2023.
  • Fewest Moves Challenge: The fewest moves challenge record is held by Sebastiano Tronto of Italy, with a solution in 15 moves, set on June 3, 2019.

Other

Along with the standard 3x3 Rubik's cube, there are other sizes for the Rubik's cube. I personally own a Rubik's cube of the size 10x10. Here are some other interesting Rubik's cube inspired puzzles!

  • Megaminx - A 10 sided inspired puzzle that is extremely difficult to solve in comparison to the standard 3x3 Rubiks cube.
  • Pyraminx - A four sided inspired puzzle that is often regarded as the easiest of this style of puzzles to solve.
  • The 1x1 - Believe it or not, there is actually a 1x1 Rubik's cube. Interestingly enough, this is a puzzle that is often regarded to be difficult to solve.
